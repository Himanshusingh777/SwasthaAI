Myth vs. Fact Section

Separating common misconceptions from evidence-based practices

Myth

Breastfeeding mothers must avoid all spicy foods

Many believe spicy foods will upset the baby through breast milk.

Fact

Most babies tolerate spicy foods in breast milk well

Research shows that most infants are not affected by moderate spice consumption in maternal diet.

Myth

Babies should sleep on their stomachs for better digestion

Some believe stomach sleeping prevents reflux and aids digestion.

Fact

Back sleeping is safest and reduces SIDS risk by 50%

Medical evidence clearly shows back sleeping is the safest position for infant sleep.

Myth

Holding babies too much will spoil them

Common belief that frequent holding creates dependency.

Fact

Responsive caregiving builds secure attachment

Research shows that responding to babies' needs promotes healthy emotional development.

Modern Guidelines
Postpartum Confinement (Jaapa)
Traditional 40-day rest period for new mothers with specific dietary guidelines and restricted activities.
Medical Perspective: Rest is beneficial, but complete isolation isn't necessary. Gentle movement and fresh air are encouraged for recovery.
Oil Massage for Babies
Daily full-body oil massage using mustard oil, coconut oil, or other traditional oils.
Medical Perspective: Gentle massage is beneficial for bonding and circulation. Use mild, tested oils and avoid if baby has skin sensitivities.
Kajal Application
Applying traditional kohl around baby's eyes for protection and aesthetics.
Medical Perspective: Commercial kajal may contain lead. If used, ensure it's certified safe and avoid application inside the eye area.
