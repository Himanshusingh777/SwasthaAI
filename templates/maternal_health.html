Complete Child Care Guide

Age-Appropriate Feeding Guidelines

0-6 Months: Exclusive Breastfeeding

  • • Feed every 2-3 hours (8-12 times daily)
  • • Watch for hunger cues: rooting, sucking motions
  • • Proper latch: baby's mouth covers most of areola
  • • Signs of adequate feeding: wet diapers, weight gain

6-12 Months: Introduction to Solids

  • • Start with single-ingredient purees
  • • Iron-rich foods: fortified cereals, pureed meats
  • • Introduce new foods one at a time (3-5 days apart)
  • • Avoid honey, cow's milk, choking hazards

12+ Months: Family Foods

  • • Transition to whole milk
  • • Finger foods and self-feeding
  • • 3 meals + 2 healthy snacks daily
  • • Limit juice, avoid added sugars

Common Health Issues & Management

Fever Management

When to call doctor: Fever >100.4°F (38°C) in infants under 3 months, or >102°F (38.9°C) in older children with concerning symptoms.

Diaper Rash

Keep area clean and dry, use barrier cream, allow air time. Consult doctor if rash spreads or shows signs of infection.

Sleep Issues

Establish routine, ensure safe sleep environment. Newborns sleep 14-17 hours, gradually decreasing with age.

Government Schemes & Benefits

Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY)

Cash assistance for institutional delivery and antenatal care services.

Benefits: ₹1,400 (rural) / ₹1,000 (urban) + transport allowance

Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana

Financial assistance of ₹5,000 for pregnant and lactating women for first live birth.

Eligibility: Pregnant women aged 19+ with family income criteria

Ayushman Bharat

Free healthcare coverage up to ₹5 lakh per family per year.

Coverage: Pregnancy, delivery, newborn care, and child health services
